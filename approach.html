Our Approach

Careful observation, controlled documentation, private review.

The goal is not to dramatize every sound or shadow. The goal is to document reported activity clearly, compare it against normal explanations, and handle the client’s privacy with care.

Core principle

We do not claim every sound, shadow, movement, or feeling is paranormal.

Investigation Process

What to expect from request to review.

01

Submit a request

Share the location type, reported activity, how long it has happened, and any immediate safety concerns.

02

Case review

The details are reviewed before deciding whether the case is appropriate and safe for the team.

03

Call or walkthrough

If needed, the team may schedule a call or walkthrough to understand the layout and activity patterns.

04

Investigation

Selected areas may be monitored with fixed video coverage and documented with careful notes.

05

Private review

Video, audio, notes, and environmental readings are reviewed privately after the investigation.

06

Permission-based sharing

Evidence is not posted publicly unless permission is given by the client or property owner.

Live Monitoring

Multi-room coverage helps compare claims with footage.

Fixed camera coverage allows selected areas to be viewed live during an investigation while footage is recorded for later review. That makes it easier to compare personal experiences with what cameras captured.

The team does not advertise live audio monitoring on the front end. The highlighted capability is live multi-camera video monitoring.

Field Standards

Professional boundaries keep cases organized.

Safety first

Not every request is a good fit. Safety, location access, and client expectations are reviewed first.

Documentation over drama

Claims are compared with recorded footage, audio review, notes, and ordinary explanations.

Confidential handling

Names, addresses, evidence, and case details are protected unless permission is granted.
